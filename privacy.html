Privacy Policy

GalleryMac · Last updated: July 8, 2026

GalleryMac is designed to keep your photos and videos entirely on your Mac. It does not collect, transmit, sell, or share any personal information, and it contains no advertising, analytics, or tracking of any kind.

Information we collect

None. GalleryMac has no accounts, no sign-in, and no servers. We do not gather usage statistics, device identifiers, crash telemetry, or any other data about you or your use of the app.

Your files

GalleryMac only accesses folders you explicitly add or open. Access is granted by you through the standard macOS file picker or drag-and-drop, and is stored on your Mac as an Apple security-scoped bookmark so your chosen folders remain available across restarts. Your photos and videos never leave your device through GalleryMac. We cannot see them, and neither can anyone else.

iCloud files

If you open a photo or video stored in iCloud Drive that hasn't been downloaded yet, GalleryMac asks macOS to download it so it can be displayed. This transfer is handled entirely by Apple's iCloud service between your Mac and your iCloud account. GalleryMac does not transmit your files to us or to any third party.

Local storage

To make browsing fast, GalleryMac stores small thumbnail previews and your app preferences in its sandboxed container on your Mac (under ~/Library/Containers/). This data stays on your device and can be removed at any time by deleting the app.

Third-party services

GalleryMac uses no third-party SDKs, advertising networks, or analytics services.

Children's privacy

GalleryMac does not collect any information from anyone, including children under 13.

Changes to this policy

If this policy changes, the updated version will be posted at this URL with a new "Last updated" date.
